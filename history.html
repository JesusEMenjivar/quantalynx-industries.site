Our History

A Decade of Industrial Innovation

2016
The Beginning

Quantalyx Industries was founded in Zurich, Switzerland by a team of robotics engineers and industrial automation specialists. Initially operating as a small robotics startup from a shared workspace, the founding team focused on developing proprietary sensor technology for manufacturing quality control. The company secured seed funding from the European Industrial Innovation Fund and established its first R&D laboratory.

During this foundational period, code-named Phase 1, the team developed the initial prototypes of what would become the Quantalyx Edge Sensor Platform. Early deployments were conducted at pilot manufacturing facilities in Bavaria and Northern Italy.

2018
Launch of Quantalyx Edge Mesh

After two years of intensive development, Quantalyx Industries launched the Quantalyx Edge Mesh - a distributed network of intelligent sensors and edge computing nodes designed specifically for factory environments. This marked the company's transition from a pure R&D operation to a commercial technology provider.

The same year, the company established its internal IT infrastructure, deploying intranet.quantalyx-industries.site for employee collaboration and devops.quantalyx-industries.site to manage continuous integration and deployment pipelines. The first version of the internal VPN gateway was deployed at vpn.quantalyx-industries.site to enable secure remote access for field engineers.

Series A funding of €12 million enabled rapid expansion, including the opening of the Munich EMEA Operations Center.

2020
Secure Manufacturing Cloud

Quantalyx Industries launched its flagship Secure Manufacturing Cloud platform, providing customers with a centralized intelligence layer for industrial operations. The platform offered AI-driven optimization, predictive maintenance analytics, and comprehensive OT/IT integration capabilities.

To support customer access, the company deployed portal.quantalyx-industries.site as the primary client interface. Enterprise single sign-on was enabled through sso.quantalyx-industries.site, integrating with customer identity providers via SAML 2.0 and OAuth protocols.

This period marked the beginning of Project Helios, an internal initiative to develop next-generation machine learning models for anomaly detection in industrial control systems. The project laid the groundwork for the advanced predictive capabilities that would differentiate Quantalyx in the market.

2023
Global Security Operations Center

Recognizing the growing threat landscape targeting industrial control systems, Quantalyx Industries established a dedicated Security Operations Center (SOC) with 24/7 monitoring capabilities. The SOC was designed to protect both Quantalyx infrastructure and customer deployments from cyber threats.

Project Aegis was launched as a comprehensive initiative to enhance security across all Quantalyx platforms. This included implementation of zero-trust networking principles, advanced SIEM integration, and the development of specialized intrusion detection systems for OT environments. Internal staff access was consolidated through staff.quantalyx-industries.site, requiring multi-factor authentication for all personnel.

The global OT monitoring network was expanded with regional nodes in Singapore, Frankfurt, and Northern Virginia, providing low-latency security monitoring for customers across all major geographic regions. The SOC team expanded to 45 analysts, with specialized units for threat intelligence, incident response, and vulnerability management.

2025
The Future of Autonomous Manufacturing

Today, Quantalyx Industries stands at the forefront of the autonomous manufacturing revolution. With operations spanning 14 countries and a client base that includes leading Tier-1 automotive manufacturers, aerospace contractors, and energy companies, the company continues to push the boundaries of what's possible in industrial automation.

The latest generation of the Secure Manufacturing Cloud platform, codenamed Project Nexus, introduces breakthrough capabilities in digital twin simulation, autonomous process optimization, and federated machine learning across distributed manufacturing networks.

Looking ahead, Quantalyx Industries remains committed to its founding mission: delivering autonomous intelligence for the next-generation factory. With continued investment in R&D at the Quantalyx Innovation Campus in Zurich and strategic partnerships across the industrial ecosystem, the company is well-positioned to shape the future of manufacturing for decades to come.
